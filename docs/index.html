The Monty Hall Problem

The "Monty Hall Problem" comes the the 70's game show "Let's Make a Deal". In the show Monty Hall (the host) would show the contestant 3 doors. Behind 1 of them was a prize, behind the other 2 were goats. This is the sequence of events that followed.

  • The contestant would choose a door
  • Then Monty would open one of the other doors which would always reveal a goat
  • Then the contestant could choose to stick with their original choice or switch doors

Most people at the time believed you should always stick with your first choice. But in 1975 someone calculated that if the contestant always decided to Switch Doors their odds of winning would basically double.

The original odds of selecting the correct door are 1/3 or about 33%. If you always switch doors the odds should be 2/3 or about 66%.
